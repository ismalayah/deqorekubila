Taylor Goldsmith Biography

Taylor Goldsmith is an American singer-songwriter, guitarist, and record producer, who is best known as the lead singer and chief songwriter of the American folk-rock band Dawes.

Goldsmith was born in Los Angeles County, California, and was raised in southern California with his younger brother Griffin by their parents Lenny and Kathy Goldsmith. His father was a musician and he toured as the lead singer of Tower of Power in the 1980s.

He went to Malibu High School where he was classmates with Blake Mills. In 2002, Goldsmith together with high school classmate and guitarist Blake Mills formed a post-punk band called Simon Dawes.

They were over the years joined by a shifting cast of bassists (Damon Webb, Grant Powell, and Wylie Gelber) and drummers (Alex Orbison, Dylan Grombocher, and Griffin Goldsmith).

The band got its debut name from the middle names of Goldsmith and Mills. They released the band’s debut album Carnivore in 2006 which received mixed reviews. The band broke up in 2007 when Blake Mills left the band.

Taylor Goldsmith And Mandy Moore

Goldsmith is a married man. He is married to Mandy Moore, an American singer-songwriter and actress.

The couple began dating in 2015. According to People, this was after Mandy shared a photo of a Dawes album on Instagram and Taylor sent her a message to say thanks. They started emailing back and forth and as they say, the rest is history.

They got engaged in late 2017 and the following year on November 18, 2018, the lovebirds got married in Los Angeles.

Taylor Goldsmith Dawes

After Blake Mills departed from the band, Goldsmith formed a new band called Dawes. He formed the and together with his former bandmate Wylie Gelber, and his younger brother Griffin Goldsmith. Also, both of Simon Dawes’ last lineup and new addition Tay Strathairn (keyboards) joined the band.

Dawes’ first album was named North Hils. It marked a sharp departure from the punk-inspired tunes of their youth. It embraced a subtler folk-rock that has been called the Laurel Canyon sound, reminiscent of Crosby, Stills, and Nash and Jackson Browne.
